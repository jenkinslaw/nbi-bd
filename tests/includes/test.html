From Event: Top 8 Title Defects - Cured., held March 2015.

Program Description

In preparation for this practical, nuts and bolts instruction, veteran title practitioners across the nation were asked what they consider the top defects they've had to resolve over the last few years. The responses were compiled and now experienced faculty is ready to take you through the list of problems most often encountered and increasingly litigated. They'll provide you with best practices for curing each one, complete with real-life scenarios and examples. Collect years of experience in resolving defects in one briefing. Order today!

Agenda / Content Covered

#8 - Break in Chain of Title
9:00 - 9:45, Presented by Anne R. Myers in Allentown, William F. Hoffmeyer in Harrisburg, and A. James Hailstone in Scranton

  • Missing Documents
  • Name Variances of the Grantor/Grantee
  • Inclusion of all Proper Parties on Documents

#7 - Rights of Third Parties
9:45 - 10:30, Presented by Anne R. Myers in Allentown, Erik M. Hume in Harrisburg, and A. James Hailstone in Scranton

  • Rights of Off-Record Third Parties
  • Right of First Refusal or Option Missed
  • Unrecorded Leases

#6 - Mortgage-Related Defects
10:45 - 11:30, Presented by Andrew J. Monastra in Allentown, Erik M. Hume in Harrisburg, and Frank D. Mroczka in Scranton

  • Improper or No Discharges of Prior Mortgages
  • Lack of Proper Assignments of the Existing Mortgage
  • Gap Mortgage with a Balance
  • Releasing Mortgages from Lenders Who Don't Exist Anymore
  • Having Possession of Adjoining Property Without a Deed

#5 - Easements/Encroachments/Setback Issues
11:30 - 12:15, Presented by Anne R. Myers in Allentown, William F. Hoffmeyer in Harrisburg, and Frank D. Mroczka in Scranton

  • Access of the Premises (Common Driveways, Private Roads, Partial Interests to Recreational Land, etc.)
  • Indefinite and Unclear Easements
  • Encroachments of a Significant Nature
  • Violation of Setback Requirements
  • Waterfront Issues Related to Reliction and Accretion
  • Railroad and Mineral Rights

#4 - Legal Description Errors
1:15 - 2:00, Presented by Andrew J. Monastra in Allentown, William F. Hoffmeyer in Harrisburg, and Frank D. Mroczka in Scranton

  • Incorrect or Incomplete Legal Description
  • Discrepancies Between Legal Description in Mortgage and Legal Description as Assessed by the City/Village/Township

#3 - Foreclosure Issues
2:15 - 3:00, Presented by Andrew J. Monastra in Allentown, Craig I. Adler in Harrisburg, and Ronald V. Santora in Scranton

  • Defective Mortgage Foreclosure
  • Failure to Notify or Serve all Necessary Parties

#2 - Estate Issues
3:00 - 3:45, Presented by Paul R. Cohen in Allentown, William F. Hoffmeyer in Harrisburg, and A. James Hailstone in Scranton

  • Missing Estate or Heir Information in the Chain of Title
  • Dower or Homestead Issues (Missing Marital Status, Missing Homestead Language, etc.)
  • Unprobated Estate Interests
  • Powers of Attorney - Improper Execution of "Fresh" POA or Defective "Old" POA
  • Life Estates Reserved

#1 - Judgments and Liens
3:45 - 4:30, Presented by Anne R. Myers in Allentown, Linda Kling in Harrisburg, and Ronald V. Santora in Scranton

  • Unsatisfied Judgment Liens Which Have or Haven't Been Paid Off
  • Failure to Obtain Releases of Mechanics' Liens
  • Springing Liens or Claimed Interests in Property

PAUL R. COHEN is a shareholder in the law firm of Jenei & Cohen, P.C., where his practice includes estate planning and administration, real estate, land use, business law, and commercial litigation. He has lectured on estate planning and real estate issues before various community and professional groups. Mr. Cohen is a faculty member of the Delaware Valley Legacy Fund; and a member of the Bucks County Estate Planning Council and the Pennsylvania and Bucks County bar associations. Mr. Cohen earned his B.A. degree, cum laude, from the State University of New York at Stony Brook; his J.D. degree, magna cum laude, from Boston University School of Law; and an estate planning certificate from Temple University Beasley School of Law's Graduate Tax Program.

ANDREW J. Mr. Monastra earned his B.S. degree, cum laude, from Villanova University; his M.B.A. degree, magna cum laude, from Drexel University; and his J.D. degree from Widener University School of Law. He is a member of the Association of Title Examiners, the Berks County Bar Association, as well as the Pennsylvania and Montgomery County bar associations. Mr. Monastra is licensed to practice law before all Pennsylvania courts, the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania, and the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit.

D. degree from Cornell University. She is a member of the Pennsylvania and Philadelphia bar associations.
